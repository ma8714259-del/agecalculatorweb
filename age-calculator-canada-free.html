Canadian Age Milestones

Canada's age-related rules are shaped by both federal law and provincial jurisdiction, meaning some milestones vary depending on where you live. Here are the key ones:

AgeCanadian Milestone
14–16Minimum driving age varies by province (14 in Alberta and Saskatchewan with a learner's stage, 16 in most others)
18–19Legal age of majority — 18 in Alberta, Manitoba, Ontario, Quebec, PEI, and Saskatchewan; 19 in the remaining provinces and territories
18Federal voting age in Canadian elections, regardless of province
18–35Eligibility window for the International Experience Canada (IEC) working holiday visa for eligible countries
60Earliest age to begin receiving CPP (Canada Pension Plan), at a reduced rate
65Standard age for full CPP benefits and eligibility for OAS (Old Age Security)
70Latest age to delay CPP for the maximum possible monthly payment
71Must convert an RRSP into a RRIF or annuity by the end of this year

Driving Age Depends on Your Province

Unlike some countries with a single national driving age, Canada leaves licensing rules to each province and territory. Alberta and Saskatchewan allow learners to begin at 14, while most other provinces set the minimum starting age at 16. Every province also uses a graduated licensing system, meaning new drivers pass through learner and intermediate stages before receiving a full licence.

CPP: Claiming Early vs Waiting

You can start collecting CPP as early as age 60, but your monthly payment is permanently reduced by 0.6% for every month before 65 that you start — up to a maximum reduction of 36%. Conversely, delaying past 65 increases your payment by 0.7% per month, up to age 70, for a maximum increase of 42%. The right choice depends heavily on your health, other income, and life expectancy expectations.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the legal age of majority in Canada?

It depends on the province: 18 in Alberta, Manitoba, Ontario, Quebec, Prince Edward Island, and Saskatchewan; 19 in British Columbia, New Brunswick, Newfoundland and Labrador, Nova Scotia, the Northwest Territories, Nunavut, and Yukon.

At what age can I start collecting CPP?

You can start as early as 60, though your monthly payment will be permanently reduced. Full CPP benefits are available at 65, and delaying until 70 gives you the maximum possible monthly amount.

What is the age limit for the IEC working holiday visa?

Most participating countries have an age range of 18 to 30 or 18 to 35, depending on your specific nationality's agreement with Canada. Always check the current eligibility for your country before applying.
