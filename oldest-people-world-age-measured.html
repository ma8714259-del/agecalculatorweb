The Oldest People in the World and How Age is Measured

May 2026 · Updated July 2026 · 7 min read · By Age Calculator Team

How old can a human being actually get? The verified record stands at 122 years and 164 days, held by Jeanne Calment of France, who passed away in 1997. Her birth was thoroughly documented by church and state records, making her the oldest verified person in human history — a record that has stood for over 25 years without being broken.

The Verified Oldest People in History

Guinness World Records maintains a strict list of verified supercentenarians — people who lived to 110 or beyond with documentation accepted as reliable. Here are some of the most well-documented cases:

Notably, the overwhelming majority of verified supercentenarians are women — the ratio is roughly 9 to 1 in most global records, a pattern researchers attribute to a combination of biological and behavioral factors that still aren't fully understood.

What Is a Supercentenarian?

People who reach 110 years or older are called supercentenarians. At any given time, only about 300–450 supercentenarians are estimated to be alive across the entire world — an extraordinarily rare group, representing roughly one in every 20 million people.

How Extreme Age Is Verified

Guinness World Records and academic gerontology researchers require multiple independent forms of documentation to verify extreme ages, including:

  • Original birth certificates or civil registration records
  • Religious records such as baptism or church registers
  • National census data from multiple decades
  • School enrollment records from childhood
  • Marriage certificates and family genealogical records
  • Testimony from surviving family members, where available

This rigorous, multi-source process exists because unverified age claims throughout history have sometimes exaggerated lifespans significantly — in some cases by decades — usually due to missing records, confusion between relatives, or in rarer cases, deliberate misrepresentation for pension or social reasons.

💡 Fact: Researchers estimate that the vast majority of historical "oldest person" claims that lack this level of documentation turn out, on closer investigation, to be significantly overstated.

The Blue Zones: Where People Live Longest

Modern research into longevity has identified several key factors associated with reaching extreme old age, including genetics, diet, social connection, regular physical activity, and a sense of purpose. Populations in regions known as Blue Zones consistently produce far higher numbers of centenarians than the global average:

  • Okinawa, Japan — known for a plant-heavy diet and strong community ties among elders
  • Sardinia, Italy — a mountainous region with unusually high rates of male centenarians
  • Nicoya, Costa Rica — noted for strong family structures and physical activity into old age
  • Ikaria, Greece — an island with notably low rates of chronic disease and dementia
  • Loma Linda, California — home to a Seventh-day Adventist community with a plant-based diet and strong social support networks

Is There a Maximum Human Lifespan?

Scientists are still divided on whether there is a hard biological ceiling on human lifespan. Some researchers argue that Jeanne Calment's record represents something close to the practical maximum given current human biology, while others believe medical advances could eventually push verified lifespans meaningfully beyond 122. What is broadly agreed upon is that reaching such extreme ages requires an unusually rare combination of genetics, environment, and simple chance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the oldest person ever verified?

Jeanne Calment of France holds the verified record at 122 years and 164 days. She passed away in 1997, and her age was confirmed through extensive documentation including birth records dating back to 1875.

How many supercentenarians are alive today?

Estimates suggest there are approximately 300 to 450 verified or likely supercentenarians (people aged 110+) alive globally at any given time, making it one of the rarest human achievements.

Why are most of the oldest verified people women?

Roughly 9 out of 10 verified supercentenarians are women. Researchers believe this reflects a combination of biological factors, such as hormonal differences affecting cardiovascular health, and behavioral factors like lower historical rates of smoking and risk-taking among women in these generations.

What are Blue Zones?

Blue Zones are specific regions around the world — including Okinawa, Sardinia, Nicoya, Ikaria, and Loma Linda — where people live measurably longer, healthier lives than the global average, attributed to diet, community, and lifestyle factors.
