Many applicants make the mistake of simply writing their age in years. However, most immigration authorities require your precise age in years, months, and sometimes even days.

Why Exact Age Matters for Visa Applications

Visa categories, fees, and eligibility thresholds are often tied to very specific age requirements. Being even one day off on your stated age can cause your application to be flagged for inconsistency, potentially leading to delays or rejection. Immigration officers verify your age against your passport and birth certificate — so accuracy is essential.

⚠️ Important: Always use your exact date of birth when completing any visa form. Never estimate or round your age — even rounding down by a few months can cause problems if the immigration system calculates differently.

Age Requirements for UK Visa Applications

The United Kingdom has several visa categories with strict age requirements. The Youth Mobility Scheme visa, for example, requires applicants to be between 18 and 30 years old at the time of application — not just during the year of application, but on the exact day the application is submitted. If you turn 31 before your application is processed, you may be ineligible.

For child visas and student visas, the UK Home Office verifies age against your passport date of birth. Our calculator gives you the exact figure you need — to the day.

Age Requirements for Canada Visa Applications

Canada's International Experience Canada (IEC) Working Holiday programme requires applicants to be between 18 and 35 years old on the day they submit their application. This is one of the strictest age-based requirements in the world of work visas. Knowing your exact age — right down to the day — is critical before applying.

Age Requirements for USA Visa Applications

The United States uses your date of birth to verify your identity across all visa categories. For ESTA (Electronic System for Travel Authorization), student visas, and tourist visas, your age is cross-referenced with your passport information. Discrepancies, however minor, can trigger additional checks that delay your travel plans.

Tips for Completing Visa Forms Accurately

  • Always double-check your date of birth against your passport before completing any form
  • Use our Age Calculator to confirm your exact age on the date of application
  • If applying for a Working Holiday visa, check your age on the specific submission date — not just the current month
  • Keep a record of your calculated age and the date you calculated it
  • If your birthday falls close to an age threshold, apply as early as possible
