How to Calculate Loan EMI Manually — Step by Step Guide

June 2026 · 5 min read · By Age Calculator Team

Calculating your loan EMI (Equated Monthly Instalment) manually helps you understand exactly how much you will pay each month before taking any loan. This guide explains the EMI formula in simple steps.

The EMI Formula

The standard EMI formula used by all banks worldwide is:

EMI = P × r × (1 + r)^n ÷ [(1 + r)^n − 1]

Where:
P = Principal loan amount
r = Monthly interest rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)
n = Total number of monthly instalments

Step-by-Step Example

Let us say you take a loan of $10,000 at 12% annual interest for 2 years (24 months).

  1. P = 10,000
  2. r = 12 ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = 0.01
  3. n = 24
  4. EMI = 10,000 × 0.01 × (1.01)^24 ÷ [(1.01)^24 − 1]
  5. EMI = $470.73 per month

What Affects Your EMI?

  • Principal Amount — Higher loan = higher EMI
  • Interest Rate — Higher rate = higher EMI
  • Loan Duration — Longer duration = lower EMI but more total interest paid

Why Calculate EMI Before Taking a Loan?

Knowing your EMI in advance helps you plan your monthly budget. Many people take loans without calculating the EMI first and then struggle with repayments. Always calculate before signing any loan agreement.

A longer loan duration reduces your monthly EMI but significantly increases the total interest you pay over time. Always choose the shortest duration you can comfortably afford.
