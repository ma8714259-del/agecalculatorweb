Loan EMI Calculator Online Free – Calculate Monthly Loan Payments

June 2026 · Updated July 2026 · 6 min read · By Age Calculator Team

An EMI, or Equated Monthly Instalment, is the fixed amount you pay each month toward a loan until it's fully repaid. Whether you're borrowing for a personal loan, a car, or a home, knowing your exact EMI before you sign helps you budget with confidence and compare offers side by side.

How EMI Is Calculated

Every bank and lender uses the same standard formula to calculate a fixed monthly instalment:

EMI = P × R × (1+R)^N ÷ [(1+R)^N − 1]
P = Principal  |  R = Monthly Interest Rate  |  N = Number of Months

Here, P is the amount you borrow, R is your monthly interest rate (your annual rate divided by 12 and by 100), and N is the total number of monthly payments over the loan term.

Step-by-Step Example

Suppose you borrow $10,000 at 10% annual interest for 3 years (36 months):

  1. Monthly rate R = 10 ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = 0.00833
  2. N = 36 months
  3. EMI = $322.67 per month
  4. Total repaid = $11,616  |  Total interest = $1,616

How Loan Term Affects Your EMI

The length of your loan changes both your monthly payment and your total interest cost. Here's how a $10,000 loan at 10% APR looks across different terms:

1 year$879.16$550

A shorter term always means a higher monthly EMI but significantly less total interest paid — while a longer term reduces your monthly burden but increases the total cost of the loan.

What Affects Your EMI Amount?

  • Loan amount — a larger principal directly increases your EMI
  • Interest rate — even a small rate difference changes your EMI meaningfully over time
  • Loan tenure — longer tenure lowers EMI but raises total interest paid
  • Repayment method — reducing balance (most common) generally results in a fairer EMI than a flat-rate calculation

Frequently Asked Questions

What does EMI stand for?

EMI stands for Equated Monthly Instalment — a fixed payment amount made by a borrower to a lender at a specified date each calendar month, covering both principal and interest.

How do I calculate EMI manually?

Use the formula EMI = P × R × (1+R)^N ÷ [(1+R)^N − 1], where P is your loan amount, R is your monthly interest rate, and N is your total number of monthly payments.

Does a longer loan term always cost more overall?

Yes. While a longer term reduces your monthly EMI, it increases the total interest you pay over the life of the loan, since interest accrues for a longer period.

Can I use this calculator for any currency?

Yes — the EMI formula works identically regardless of currency. Simply enter your loan amount, rate, and term in your local currency for an accurate result.
