Driving Age Varies by State

Unlike many countries with a single national driving age, the United States leaves this decision to individual states. Some states allow a learner's permit as early as 14 (such as South Dakota), while others require applicants to be 16 before even starting the process. Always check your specific state's DMV requirements rather than assuming a national standard applies.

Social Security: Claiming Early vs Waiting

You can start claiming Social Security retirement benefits as early as 62, but doing so permanently reduces your monthly payment compared to waiting until your full retirement age (66 to 67, depending on birth year). Delaying benefits even further, up to age 70, increases your monthly payment beyond the full amount — a factor worth calculating carefully based on your health and financial situation.

Frequently Asked Questions

At what age can I get a driving licence in the USA?

This depends entirely on your state. Most states allow a full unrestricted licence between 16 and 18, though learner's permits can often be obtained one to two years earlier.

When am I eligible for Medicare?

Medicare eligibility begins at age 65 for most Americans, regardless of retirement status, as long as you or your spouse have sufficient work history paying into the system.

What is the earliest age I can claim Social Security?

You can claim as early as age 62, but your monthly benefit will be permanently reduced compared to waiting until your full retirement age of 66 to 67.
