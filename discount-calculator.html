Discount Calculator – Free Sale Price & Percent Off Calculator

This discount calculator instantly works out the sale price, the amount you save, and the final total after tax on any percent-off deal. Whether you're shopping a Black Friday sale, comparing coupon codes, or stacking two discounts on top of each other, this tool does the maths for you in one click — no signup needed, works entirely in your browser.

Stacked discounts don't simply add together — a 20% discount followed by a 10% discount isn't 30% off, it's actually 28% off, because the second discount applies to the already-reduced price. Toggle "Stacked Discounts" above to see the true combined savings, along with the effective (real) discount percentage.

How do you calculate a discount?
To find a sale price, multiply the original price by (1 − discount ÷ 100). For example, $80 at 25% off is $80 × 0.75 = $60. This calculator does that instantly and also shows exactly how much you saved.
Do two discounts add up, like 20% + 10% = 30% off?
No — stacked discounts are applied one after another, not added together. A 20% discount followed by a 10% discount on the reduced price works out to a 28% total discount, not 30%. Use the "Stacked Discounts" mode above to calculate this correctly.
Should tax be calculated before or after the discount?
In most places, including the US, UK, and Canada, sales tax or VAT is calculated on the discounted price, not the original price. This calculator applies tax after the discount by default, matching standard checkout practice.
Is this a percent off calculator too?
Yes. Enter your original price and the percentage off, and this calculator works as both a discount calculator and a percent off calculator, showing the sale price and the amount saved side by side.
Is my data stored or shared?
Never. All calculations happen entirely in your browser. No personal data is collected, stored, or sent to any server.
