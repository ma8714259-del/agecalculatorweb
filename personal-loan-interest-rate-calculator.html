Personal Loan Interest Rate Comparison — USA, UK & Canada

Updated July 2026 · 6 min read · By Age Calculator Team

The interest rate you're offered for a personal loan can vary enormously depending on where you live, which type of lender you approach, and your financial profile. This guide compares typical rate ranges across the main lender categories in the USA, UK, and Canada, so you know what to expect before you apply.

Typical Rate Ranges by Lender Type (USA)

Lender TypeTypical APR RangeNotes
Traditional Banks10% – 18%Best for existing customers with strong history
Credit Unions8% – 15%Often lower rates but membership required
Online Lenders6% – 25%Fast approval, wide range depending on credit score

Typical Rate Ranges (Canada)

Lender TypeTypical APR RangeNotes
Big Five Banks7% – 16%Slightly better rates for larger loan amounts
Credit Unions6% – 13%Often the cheapest option if eligible
Online Lenders8% – 30%Convenient but generally the most expensive tier

Rate ranges shift with central bank policy, so treat these as general guidance rather than guaranteed numbers. Always check the current advertised APR directly with the lender before applying.

Why the Same Borrower Gets Different Offers

Two people with identical income can receive very different rate offers from the same lender. This happens because lenders also weigh existing relationship history, the exact loan purpose, whether the application is joint or individual, and how many other loans or credit lines the applicant currently holds.

How to Actually Compare Offers Fairly

  1. Always compare the APR (which includes fees), not just the headline interest rate
  2. Get quotes from at least one bank, one credit union, and one online lender
  3. Use a soft-search or pre-qualification tool where available, so comparing doesn't hurt your credit score
  4. Check for early repayment penalties — a slightly higher rate with no penalty can cost less overall
