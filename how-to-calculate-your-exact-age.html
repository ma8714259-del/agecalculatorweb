How to Calculate Your Exact Age in Years, Months and Days

May 2026  ·  4 min read  ·  By Age Calculator Team

Most people know their age in years — but do you know your age in exact months, days, hours and seconds? Calculating your precise age is more complex than it sounds, mainly because of leap years, varying month lengths, and time zones.

The Basic Method

To calculate your age, you subtract your date of birth from today's date. Simple subtraction gives you years. But to get months and days accurately, you need to account for the fact that months have different numbers of days — February has 28 or 29 days, while July and August both have 31.

For example, if you were born on 15 March 2000, and today is 15 June 2026, your age is exactly 26 years, 3 months, and 0 days. But if today were 10 June 2026, your age would be 26 years, 2 months, and 26 days — not 3 months.

Why Leap Years Matter

Every 4 years, February gains an extra day — making it 29 days instead of 28. This is called a leap year and it exists because the Earth takes approximately 365.25 days to orbit the Sun. Without leap years, our calendar would slowly drift out of sync with the seasons.

🗓️ Fun fact: If you were born on February 29th (a leap day), your actual birthday only occurs every 4 years! There are approximately 5 million people in the world with a leap day birthday.

Manual Calculation — Step by Step

If you want to calculate your age manually, here is how to do it correctly:

  1. Start with years: Subtract your birth year from the current year.
  2. Check the month: If your birth month has not yet passed this year, subtract one year.
  3. Check the day: If it is your birth month but your birthday has not yet passed, subtract one more year.
  4. Calculate months: Count months from your last birthday to today.
  5. Calculate days: Count remaining days after the last complete month.

Why Exact Age Matters

Your exact age matters far more than most people realise. Here are some situations where precision is critical:

  • Passport and visa applications — many countries issue different documents based on exact age thresholds
  • School admissions — intake cutoff dates are based on your age to the day
  • Medical screenings — health programmes are triggered by precise age milestones
  • Legal rights — voting, driving, and signing contracts all have exact age requirements
  • Pension eligibility — state pension age is calculated to the exact day in most countries

Age in Seconds — The Fascinating Numbers

One year contains approximately 31,536,000 seconds. A leap year adds another 86,400 seconds. So if you are 26 years old, you have lived over 820 million seconds — an astonishing number that makes life feel precious and vast at the same time.
