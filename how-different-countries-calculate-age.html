How Different Countries Calculate Age Differently

May 2026 · 4 min read · By Age Calculator Team

Most people assume that age is calculated the same way everywhere in the world. In reality, different cultures have very different systems — and some may surprise you considerably.

The Western System

In most Western countries including the UK, USA, Australia and Europe, age is counted from the moment of birth. You are zero years old until your first birthday, when you turn one. This is the most widely used system internationally.

The East Asian System

In traditional Korean, Chinese and Vietnamese cultures, a baby is considered to be one year old at birth. The time spent in the womb counts as the first year of life. In South Korea, this traditional system was officially used in official contexts until 2023, when the government standardised the Western system for legal purposes.

The Japanese System

Japan historically used a system called kazoedoshi, where everyone became one year older on New Year's Day regardless of their actual birthday. This meant two people born on December 31st and January 1st could be the same age for most of the year but differ by two kazoedoshi years immediately after New Year. This system is now rarely used in modern Japan.
