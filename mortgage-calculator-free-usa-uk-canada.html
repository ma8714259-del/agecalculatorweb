What Is a Mortgage Calculator?

A mortgage calculator is a free online tool that tells you your exact monthly repayment based on three inputs — the loan amount, interest rate, and loan term. It uses the same formula that every bank worldwide uses to calculate mortgage payments.

Use it before you speak to any lender — so you already know what to expect before they give you a number.

How to Calculate Mortgage Payments

Every bank uses this standard formula to calculate your monthly mortgage payment:

M = P × r(1+r)^n ÷ [(1+r)^n – 1]
M = Monthly Payment  |  P = Loan Amount  |  r = Monthly Rate (Annual ÷ 12 ÷ 100)  |  n = Total Months

Step-by-Step Example (USA)

Home price $400,000 — Down payment $80,000 — Rate 6.53% — 30 years:

  1. Loan amount = $400,000 − $80,000 = $320,000
  2. Monthly rate r = 6.53 ÷ 12 ÷ 100 = 0.005442
  3. n = 30 × 12 = 360 months
  4. Monthly payment = $2,024
  5. Total paid = $729,000  |  Total interest = $409,000

Mortgage Calculator UK — How It Works

In the United Kingdom, mortgages work slightly differently from the USA:

  • Standard term: 25 years (30–35 year terms now common for first-time buyers)
  • Rate type: Most borrowers choose 2-year or 5-year fixed deals, then remortgage
  • Current rates: 2-year fixed deals average around 4.8% in mid-2026
  • Deposit: 10% minimum; 20%+ gets the best rates
  • Stamp Duty: Additional tax on purchase — factor this into your budget

UK Example

Property price £300,000 — 10% deposit £30,000 — Rate 4.8% — 25 years:

  • Loan = £270,000  |  Monthly payment = £1,541
  • Total paid = £462,000  |  Total interest = £192,000

Mortgage Calculator Canada — What You Need to Know

In Canada, the mortgage system has some unique rules:

  • Stress test: You must qualify at your contract rate + 2% (or 5.25%, whichever is higher)
  • CMHC insurance: Required if your down payment is less than 20%
  • Standard term: 25-year amortization, 5-year fixed rate most common
  • Current rates: 5-year fixed mortgages average around 5.1% in mid-2026
  • Minimum down payment: 5% for homes under $500,000 CAD

7 Ways to Get a Lower Mortgage Rate

  1. Improve your credit score — even 20 extra points can save 0.5% on your rate.
  2. Save a larger down payment — 20% eliminates PMI and usually gets a better rate.
  3. Compare multiple lenders — rates can vary by 0.5%–1.0% between lenders.
  4. Choose a shorter term — 15-year rates are typically 0.5%–0.75% lower than 30-year.
  5. Buy points — pay upfront to permanently lower your interest rate.
  6. Lock your rate — when you find a good rate, lock it before it rises.
  7. Use a mortgage broker — they have access to multiple lenders and can negotiate on your behalf.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my monthly mortgage payment?
Use the formula M = P × r(1+r)^n ÷ [(1+r)^n – 1], where P is your loan amount, r is your monthly rate, and n is total months. Or use the free mortgage calculator above — enter your home price, down payment, rate, and term for an instant result.
What is a good mortgage interest rate in 2026?
In the USA, the 30-year fixed average is around 6.5% in mid-2026. In the UK, 2-year fixed deals average 4.8%. In Canada, 5-year fixed rates are around 5.1%. Anything below your country's average for your credit score tier is considered a good rate.
How much mortgage can I afford?
The 28% rule says your monthly mortgage payment should not exceed 28% of your gross monthly income. If you earn $6,000 per month, your maximum monthly payment should be around $1,680.
Do I need a 20% down payment?
No — in the USA you can get a mortgage with as little as 3% down, and in the UK with 5% deposit. However, a 20% down payment gets you the best rates and avoids mortgage insurance fees in every country.
Is a 30-year or 15-year mortgage better?
A 15-year mortgage has higher monthly payments but you pay far less total interest — often half as much. A 30-year mortgage gives lower monthly payments and more cash flow flexibility. Use the calculator above to compare both options for your loan amount.
Can I use this calculator for UK pounds or Canadian dollars?
Yes — the calculation is the same regardless of currency. Enter your home price in your local currency (GBP, CAD, AUD, EUR) and use the country preset button to load the current average rate for your region.
What is the difference between fixed and variable rate mortgage?
A fixed rate locks in your interest rate for the full term so your payment never changes. A variable rate moves with the market — it may start lower but can increase if central banks raise rates. Fixed gives certainty; variable offers potential savings.
