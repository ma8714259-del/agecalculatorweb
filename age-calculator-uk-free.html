UK-Specific Age Milestones

The United Kingdom has its own set of legally significant ages that differ in some respects from other countries. Here are the key ones:

AgeUK Milestone
4–5Compulsory school starting age (varies slightly by nation within the UK)
16Can leave school, get married with parental consent, and pay full adult NHS prescription charges
17Minimum age to hold a full UK car driving licence
18Legal adulthood — voting age, right to buy alcohol, and full contractual capacity
22Automatic enrolment into a workplace pension typically begins (if earning above the threshold)
25Universal Credit standard allowance increases to the full adult rate
50NHS invites women for routine breast cancer screening (bowel screening starts around this age too)
60Free NHS prescriptions begin in England (already free in Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland at all ages)
66Current State Pension age (rising to 67 by 2028)
70Upper age for routine NHS breast screening invitations (self-referral still available after)

UK Passport and Travel Age Rules

UK passports for adults are valid for 10 years from the date of issue, while child passports (for those under 16) are valid for only 5 years. Children require their own passport for international travel from birth — there is no minimum age requirement to hold one.

State Pension Age Is Changing

The UK State Pension age is not fixed forever — it is currently 66 for both men and women, but is scheduled to rise to 67 between 2026 and 2028, and a further rise to 68 is under government review for the late 2030s or 2040s. Anyone planning retirement should check their exact State Pension age using the official government calculator, since it depends precisely on your date of birth.

Frequently Asked Questions

At what age can I get a full UK driving licence?

You can apply for a provisional licence at 15 years and 9 months and take your driving test from age 17, at which point you can hold a full UK car licence.

When do I get free NHS prescriptions in the UK?

In England, free NHS prescriptions begin automatically at age 60. In Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland, prescriptions are free for everyone regardless of age.

What is the current UK State Pension age?

The State Pension age is currently 66 for both men and women, but is rising to 67 between 2026 and 2028. Your exact date depends on your date of birth.
