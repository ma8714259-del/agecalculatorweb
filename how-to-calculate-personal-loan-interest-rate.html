🧮 MANUAL CALCULATION

How to Calculate Personal Loan Interest Rate Manually (With Formulas)

Updated July 2026 · 7 min read · By Age Calculator Team

Every bank quotes an interest rate, but very few explain the actual math behind it. This guide walks through the exact formulas used to calculate personal loan interest by hand — no calculator app needed — so you can verify any loan offer yourself with just a pen, paper, and a basic calculator app on your phone.

"If you can't calculate it yourself, you're trusting the lender's math blindly."

1. Flat Rate Method — By Hand

Total Interest = (Principal × Rate × Time) / 100

This is the simplest formula. Multiply the loan amount by the annual rate and the number of years, then divide by 100. For example, a loan of 300,000 at 10% for 2 years gives: (300,000 × 10 × 2) / 100 = 60,000 total interest, regardless of how much principal you've already repaid.

2. Reducing Balance Method — Step by Step

This is the method almost all personal loan lenders actually use. Instead of one formula, it requires calculating interest month by month on the remaining balance:

Monthly Interest = Outstanding Balance × (Annual Rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100)

In month 1, interest is calculated on the full loan amount. In month 2, it's calculated only on what's left after your first payment reduces the principal. This is why the reducing balance method always costs less than a flat rate loan at the same advertised percentage.

3. The EMI Formula (For Fixed Monthly Payments)

EMI = [P × R × (1+R)^N] / [(1+R)^N − 1]

Where P = principal, R = monthly interest rate (annual ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and N = number of months. This formula looks intimidating, but it simply solves for a fixed monthly payment that fully repays the loan plus interest by the final month.

5. Quick Sanity Check

Once you've calculated your EMI manually, multiply it by the number of months and subtract the original principal — the result should roughly match your total interest. If a lender's quoted total interest is significantly higher than your manual calculation, ask them to explain the discrepancy before signing.
