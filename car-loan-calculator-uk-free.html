Example: £15,000 Car Loan in UK

If you borrow £15,000 at 8.9% APR for 4 years:

  • Monthly payment: approximately £373
  • Total amount paid: approximately £17,904
  • Total interest paid: approximately £2,904

In the UK, always check the APR (Annual Percentage Rate) rather than just the interest rate. The APR includes all fees and gives you the true cost of borrowing.

Current UK Car Finance Rates (2026)

  • Best credit score: 5% – 8% APR
  • Good credit score: 8% – 15% APR
  • Fair credit score: 15% – 25% APR
  • Poor credit score: 25% – 40%+ APR

How to Get the Best Car Loan Deal in UK

  1. Check your credit score on Experian, Equifax or TransUnion
  2. Compare deals from multiple lenders — not just the dealership
  3. Put down a larger deposit to reduce monthly payments
  4. Choose a shorter loan term to pay less interest overall
  5. Consider 0% finance deals from manufacturers if eligible

UK Car Finance Tips

  • Section 75 of the Consumer Credit Act protects purchases over £100
  • You can settle a finance deal early (may have early repayment charges)
  • Voluntary termination rights apply after you have paid 50% of total amount

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I calculate my car loan payment in the UK?

Enter the car price, your deposit, the APR offered by your lender, and the loan term in the calculator above. It instantly shows your monthly payment, total interest, and a full month-by-month amortization schedule.

What is a good interest rate for a car loan in the UK?

In 2026, a good car loan APR in the UK ranges from 5% to 8% for buyers with excellent credit. Average rates across all credit profiles typically fall between 8% and 25%, depending on the lender and credit history.

How much deposit do I need for a car loan in the UK?

Most UK lenders recommend a deposit of at least 10% of the car's value. A larger deposit reduces your monthly payment and can help you qualify for a better APR.
